Yibu

Yibu is a contextually aware and generated game with real world challenges. It uses sensors as tools to play with invisible data that surrounds us everyday, turning every home in an expanded playground for hidden and playful adventures.

As part of frogLabs initiatives, I had the chance to create a project and run a team to explore new ways of using sensing technology to go beyond quantification of our environment.

We use sensors to explore and discover the environment around us, to feel more connected to our context and to find ways control it. We use them to count, control, measure and mostly visualise it as numbers and graphs. But what if we could transform sesnors into something that allows you to explore and play? What if instead of a graph with numbers on a screen we would turn sensing into a game or a story to follow and solve?

We started our process by thinkering and prototyping directly with environmental sensors. In a 5-weeks iterative process we went from early ideas and sketches to a working prototype of a character, a story and a game that we could test with kids.

We turned 5 sensors into wooden toys to play with temperature, light, sound, direction and wind. Using these sensors kids solve challenges by creating a better, healthier, favourable environment for a character that lives in a digital world.
